Equitable, low-impact transport for all

Car dependency fuels climate change, divides communities, and makes cities unlivable. This policy ensures transport serves people, not just vehicles.

body: >-

Policy Objective 1 - Equitable, low-impact transport for all

Car dependency fuels climate change, divides communities, and makes cities unlivable. This policy ensures transport serves people, not just vehicles.

Urban Planning Policy Instruments 👇

Remember: Policy instruments are the means by which governments can achieve the degrowth goals/objectives we urgently need!

1 - Reduce fossil-fuel and motorised mobility 🚗

💡 Translation: Make public and active transport like cycling and walking the default, not cars and planes🚫 Limit automobiles at city level, reduce speed limits, and close city centres to cars.🚗 Tax private vehicles heavily, especially SUVs.🚍 Expand and subsidise public transport and cargo bikes.

Policy Objective 2 - Promote modal shift to active and public transport (e.g. walking, cycling, bus, trolley bus, metro, rail)
1 - Promote a shift to walking, cycling, and public transport 🚲

💡 Translation: Cities should prioritise human movement, not just vehicle flow.🚴 Build high-quality cycling and pedestrian infrastructure.🚆 Expand trolleybuses, metros, and rail systems instead of roads.🏙 Design compact, walkable cities where people don’t need cars.

Policy Objective 3 - Limit high-speed transport infrastructure
1 - Limit high-speed transport infrastructure 🚙

💡 Translation: Stop building mega-projects that fuel sprawl and destruction.✈️ Reduce investment in highways, airports, and high-speed trains that drive urban sprawl.🚧 Protest unnecessary large-scale transport projects that destroy ecosystems.💥 Mobility should be about accessibility and sustainability, not endless expansion.
