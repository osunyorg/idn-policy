Policy Objective 1 - Ensuring ethical, sustainable, and equitable land use

Land shouldn’t be a commodity to speculate on—it’s a shared resource for living, growing food, and building communities.

Urban Planning Policy Instruments 👇

Remember: Policy instruments are the means by which governments can achieve the degrowth goals/objectives we urgently need!

1 - Restrict the commodification of property 🏘️

💡 Translation: Housing is a human right, not an investment opportunity.🏛 Introduce progressive property taxes to prevent hoarding and speculation.🏚 Enforce rent caps, limits to private property, and quotas on floor area per capita.🏘 Put vacant buildings and land into community land trusts.

Policy Objective 2 - Increase social housing
1 - Increase access to land for community use 🏞️

💡 Translation: Cities should prioritise people and food, not just buildings and businesses.🌱 Ensure land for urban agriculture, community gardens, and food forests.🏡 Convert underutilised public land into spaces for community-led ecological projects.

2 - Enforce land redistribution policies 👮🏾

💡 Translation: Reverse historical land injustices and give land back to those who need it.🌍 Redistribute land to indigenous communities and those displaced by urban development.🏞 End land grabs and ensure equitable land access for all.💥 Land should serve people, not be hoarded for profit.
